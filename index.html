Get GameDev here

Serious message from me to you on success in the Humber's Game Programming Stream:

It's crazy important for you all to understand that learning to program requires practice, effort and mileage (time).

Copying code from others or taking shortcuts may help you pass a class in the short term, but it won't give you a solid foundation of knowledge and skills. In the long run, this will hinder your ability to succeed in the field of game programming and damage your future career prospects.

You really need to put in the time and effort to truly understand the concepts and practices being taught in this program. If you're struggling, please don't be afraid to ask for help. Your instructors are here to support you and want you to succeed. However, it's also important to show a genuine effort and commitment to learning. If it appears that you're not putting in the time or taking shortcuts, your instructors may not be as willing to provide additional support or their additional time.

Trust me.
